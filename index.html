FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about decentralized hosting with Web3Deploy.

What is IPFS?

IPFS (InterPlanetary File System) is a peer-to-peer network protocol designed to make the web faster, safer, and more open. Instead of relying on centralized servers, IPFS distributes your content across a decentralized network of nodes, making it censorship-resistant and permanently accessible.

Is my data safe?

Yes. Your API keys are stored exclusively in your browser's localStorage and never leave your device. All uploads go directly from your browser to the provider's API — we don't proxy, log, or store any of your data or credentials.

What happens when I reach the storage limit?

Each provider has its own free tier limit. When you reach it, you can either upgrade to a paid plan directly with the provider, switch to a different provider, or unpin old files to free up space. Web3Deploy makes it easy to manage multiple providers from one dashboard.

How do I connect my own domain?

After uploading, you can link a custom domain by setting a DNSLink TXT record pointing to your IPFS content hash. We provide step-by-step instructions in the dashboard. Alternatively, you can use services like Fleek or Cloudflare to map your domain to your IPFS deployment.
